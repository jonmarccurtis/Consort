Explanation of Voice Parts

Directors assign the Singer's Voice Parts for each song.  We start with a "Primary Voice Part" like S1 or B2 which used to determine our default voice parts for most of the songs.  Your primary voice part is listed in the upper-right corner.  If this is not correct, please let one of the Directors or Note Takers know.

There are often additional assignments for all, or part, of some songs (e.g. Middle voices, or moving S2 to A1.)  Often a piece may have only a few sections of 3 way split using middle voices.  Some of those middle voices will be singing S2 and others A1 when outside of these sections.  The assignments are always "inclusive", meaning that all voice parts used in a song will be listed for the singer.  Some will be 'S2,um' and others will be 'um,A1'.  (Middle voices are always upper (women) or lower (men) = 'um' or 'lm' so that we can identify which is which.)

Another anomaly is when a song is SAA but the Director wants it to be sung with High, Middle, Low voices in a 3 way split.  In cases like this, the song's voice parts will follow what is written in the score.  High voices will be assigned S, Middle voices A1, and Low A2.  This way your assignment will match how it is written in the score.
